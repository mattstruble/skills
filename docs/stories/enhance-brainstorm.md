# Story: Enhance brainstorm with 80/20 Thinking

## Source
Brainstorm: Grug Brain Philosophy Integration
Behaviors covered:
- 80/20 thinking as complement to YAGNI: when you can't say no, find the minimal version

## Summary
Add 80/20 thinking to the brainstorm skill as a second move after YAGNI. Currently the skill only has the "no" hammer for complexity. This adds the "ok, but here's the minimal version" approach for when features survive the YAGNI challenge due to legitimate business constraints.

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] 80/20 thinking is added as a named concept that complements the existing "YAGNI ruthlessly" guidance
- [ ] Positioned in the questioning phase: when a feature survives the YAGNI challenge, the next question becomes "what's the 80/20 version?"
- [ ] Framing is concrete: deliver 80% of the value with 20% of the complexity. Strip to the version that satisfies the core need without bells and whistles
- [ ] Acknowledges the pragmatic context: sometimes you can't say no (business requirements, stakeholder demands, team dynamics) and that's okay -- the goal shifts from elimination to simplification
- [ ] Does not weaken the existing YAGNI stance -- 80/20 is the fallback when YAGNI fails, not a replacement
